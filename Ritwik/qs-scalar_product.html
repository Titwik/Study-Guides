The Scalar Product: Questions

Author

Ritwik Anand

Summary
A selection of questions for the study guide on the Scalar Product

Before attempting these questions, it is highly recommended that you read Guide: The Scalar Product, as well as the Guide: Introduction to Quadratic Equations.

Questions

Q1

Find the scalar product of u and v.

1.1. u \(= \begin{pmatrix}6\\3\\4\end{pmatrix}\) and v \(= \begin{pmatrix}1\\4\\2\end{pmatrix}\)

1.2. u \(= \begin{pmatrix}10\\-7\\4\end{pmatrix}\) and v \(= \begin{pmatrix}3\\-5\\13\end{pmatrix}\)

1.3. u \(= \begin{pmatrix}-4.4\\-1.2\\0.3\end{pmatrix}\) and v \(= \begin{pmatrix}6.1\\-2.5\\9.3\end{pmatrix}\)

1.4. u \(= \begin{pmatrix}54\\38\\0\end{pmatrix}\) and v \(= \begin{pmatrix}32\\-55\\13\end{pmatrix}\)

1.5. u \(= 2\mathbf{i} + 7\mathbf{j} + \mathbf{k}\) and v \(= 6\mathbf{i} + 4\mathbf{j} + 8\mathbf{k}\)

1.6. u \(= -3\mathbf{i} + 10\mathbf{j} - 8\mathbf{k}\) and v \(= \mathbf{i} - 12\mathbf{j} + 9\mathbf{k}\)

1.7. u \(= 17\mathbf{j} + 23\mathbf{k}\) and v \(= 6\mathbf{i} - 23\mathbf{j} - 8\mathbf{k}\)

1.8. u \(= \mathbf{i}\) and v \(= \mathbf{j}\).

What can you say about the result of (1.8.)? Can you deduce similar conclusions for the scalar product of different combinations of the base vectors \(\mathbf{i}\), \(\mathbf{j}\), \(\mathbf{k}\)?

Q2

Find the value(s) of \(\lambda\) for which u and v are perpendicular.

2.1. u \(= \begin{pmatrix}2\\4\\7\end{pmatrix}\) and v \(= \begin{pmatrix}1\\\lambda\\-2\end{pmatrix}\)

2.2. u \(= \begin{pmatrix}0\\1\\\lambda\end{pmatrix}\) and v \(= \begin{pmatrix}1\\2\\3\end{pmatrix}\)

2.3. u \(= \begin{pmatrix}9\\-2\\11\end{pmatrix}\) and v \(= \begin{pmatrix}\lambda\\-\lambda\\3\end{pmatrix}\)

2.4. u \(= \begin{pmatrix}\lambda\\6\\1\end{pmatrix}\) and v \(= \begin{pmatrix}\lambda\\\lambda\\8\end{pmatrix}\)

2.5. u \(= \begin{pmatrix}-2\lambda^2\\4\\14\end{pmatrix}\) and v \(= \begin{pmatrix}3\\2\lambda\\1\end{pmatrix}\)

2.6. u \(= \begin{pmatrix}-5\\9\\2\lambda\end{pmatrix}\) and v \(= \begin{pmatrix}\lambda\\-2\\\lambda\end{pmatrix}\)

2.7. u \(= \begin{pmatrix}-7\\4\\2\lambda\end{pmatrix}\) and v \(= \begin{pmatrix}2\lambda\\1\\6\lambda\end{pmatrix}\)

2.8. u \(= \begin{pmatrix}-25\\-\lambda^2\\-2\end{pmatrix}\) and v \(= \begin{pmatrix}3\lambda\\-11\\7\end{pmatrix}\)

Q3

3.2. u \(= \begin{pmatrix}1\\1\\1\end{pmatrix}\) and v \(= \begin{pmatrix}1\\-1\\1\end{pmatrix}\)

3.3. u \(= \begin{pmatrix}-8\\1\\-4\end{pmatrix}\) and v \(= \begin{pmatrix}-1\\-5\\7\end{pmatrix}\)

3.4. u \(= \begin{pmatrix}1.2\\-1.4\\-3.1\end{pmatrix}\) and v \(= \begin{pmatrix}-5.4\\9.7\\-7.5\end{pmatrix}\)

3.5. u \(= \begin{pmatrix}45\\65\\54\end{pmatrix}\) and v \(= \begin{pmatrix}-19\\-58\\71\end{pmatrix}\)

3.6. u \(= \begin{pmatrix}1\\0\\0\end{pmatrix}\) and v \(= \begin{pmatrix}0\\0\\1\end{pmatrix}\)

Q4
